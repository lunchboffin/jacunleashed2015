The idea

Our (very good) idea was to develop a fun and interactive game using open data.
Our game involves the player controlling a duck

Hackerspace Page

The datasets

For shipwreck locations and data about shipwrecks: Shipwrecks
For on-land locations of interest: SA History Hub GEOJSON - Historical Places
For on-land locations of interest in the Port Adelaide area: Points of Interest in the City of Port Adelaide Enfield
For state borders (used for the in-game minimap): GEODATA COAST 100K 2004
For land/water boundaries (used for the rivers and lakes in the in-game minimap, and governs the duck swimming versus flying animation): Prescribed Watercourses
For in-game music, we chose The Viceroy Tea Waltz, by Alberto Agrati, found from the following dataset: South Australian Sheet Music 1852-1927

The process

We started with an idea for a shipwreck explorer game that would use the Shipwrecks dataset on data.sa.gov.au provided by the Department of Environment, Water and Natural Resources. From there we built a few early prototypes (Bad screenshot here) (another bad screenshot here)

The outcome

A working prototype game with playable demo using a total of five datasets from data.sa.gov.au and one from data.gov.au.

The audience

Our game is designed for all ages, it allows players to explore South Australia and discover many interesting places and shipwrecks while also providing interesting information about each place. Our game is a great way for young children to engauge with the history of South Australia in a fun and unique way.
